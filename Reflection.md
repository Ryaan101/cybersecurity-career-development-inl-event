# Reflection

## Activity Description
For this career development experience, I attended a Cyber Security Group (CSG) event at Washington State University that featured researchers and recruiters from Idaho National Laboratory (INL). The purpose of the event was to provide insight into cybersecurity careers, research opportunities, and internship pathways. During the event, I listened to presentations, took notes, and learned about the type of work INL is involved in, including cybersecurity, artificial intelligence, and critical infrastructure systems. I also gained a better understanding of what employers are looking for in students applying to internships.

## Technical and Professional Decisions
One of the main decisions I made was to attend this event instead of only applying to internships without preparation. I wanted to better understand what companies like INL expect before submitting applications. From the event, I learned the importance of building a strong foundation in technical skills, gaining hands-on experience, and being able to clearly communicate my interests and abilities. Based on this, I decided to improve my resume, organize my GitHub projects, and tailor my application materials specifically toward cybersecurity and systems-related roles.

## Contributions
This was an individual activity. My contributions included actively attending the event, taking detailed notes, and reflecting on how the information applied to my own career path. I also took action afterward by updating my resume, creating a structured GitHub repository for this assignment, and preparing application materials for the INL internship. These steps allowed me to turn what I learned from the event into something practical and useful.

## Quality Assessment
I believe I performed well in this activity because I was fully engaged and used the experience to guide my next steps. I did not just attend the event, but also applied what I learned to improve my professional materials. However, if I were to improve, I would make a greater effort to directly network with the recruiters and ask more specific questions. This would help me build stronger connections and stand out more in future opportunities.

## Career Alignment
This experience directly supports my career goals of working in cybersecurity. It helped me understand how cybersecurity applies to real-world systems, especially in areas like national security and critical infrastructure. It also gave me a clearer idea of the skills and experiences I need to develop in order to be a strong candidate for internships. Overall, this experience moved me closer to my career goals by helping me take concrete steps toward gaining hands-on experience and preparing for opportunities like the INL internship.
